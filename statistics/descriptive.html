Descriptive Statistics

This page contains practice questions covering levels of data and descriptive statistics.

1. Below is a questionnaire that is given to customers who visit the “Pecs Bar & Gym”. Determine the level of data (Nominal, Ordinal, Scale) produced for each of the questions.

  1. Do you use a car to come to the gym? (Yes / No)

    Show Solution

    The answers are distinct categories with no inherent order.

    Answer: Nominal

  2. How would you rate the cleanliness of our facilities? (Excellent, Good, Average, Poor)

    Show Solution

    The answers have a meaningful order, but the intervals between them are not equal.

    Answer: Ordinal

  3. What distance, in km, do you live from the gym?

    Show Solution

    The data is numerical, has equal intervals, and a true zero point.

    Answer: Scale (Ratio)

  4. How many times did you visit the gym last month?

    Show Solution

    The data is a numerical count with equal intervals and a true zero.

    Answer: Scale (Ratio)

  5. Which of these services did you use on your last visit? (Free Weights, Cardio Machines, Fitness Class, Bar)

    Show Solution

    The answers are distinct categories with no inherent order.

    Answer: Nominal

3. The gym is interested in how long it takes people to get to the gym. They conduct a survey of 8 people... The sample results were: 12, 15, 16, 18, 20, 21, 22, 28

  1. Calculate the mean time.

    Show Solution

    Sum of values: $12+15+16+18+20+21+22+28 = 152$.
    Divide by the number of values (n=8): $152 \div 8$.

    Answer: 19 minutes

  2. Calculate the median time.

    Show Solution

    The data is already ordered. The middle two values are the 4th and 5th: 18 and 20.
    The median is the average of these two: $(18+20) \div 2$.

    Answer: 19 minutes

  3. Calculate the range of times.

    Show Solution

    Highest value minus the lowest value: $28 - 12$.

    Answer: 16 minutes

  4. Use the formula $s = \sqrt{\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}$ to calculate the sample standard deviation.

    1. Write down the 8 times with the mean subtracted from each of them ($x-\bar{x}$).

      Show Solution

      Answer: -7, -4, -3, -1, 1, 2, 3, 9

    2. Square each of the 8 values from part i ($(x-\bar{x})^2$).

      Show Solution

      Answer: 49, 16, 9, 1, 1, 4, 9, 81

    3. Add all the values from part ii together to find $\sum(x-\bar{x})^2$.

      Show Solution

      $49 + 16 + 9 + 1 + 1 + 4 + 9 + 81$.

      Answer: 170

    4. Divide your answer to part iii by $n-1$.

      Show Solution

      $170 \div (8-1) = 170 \div 7$.

      Answer: 24.29 (approx)

    5. What statistical name is given to the value calculated in part iv?

      Show Solution

      Answer: The sample variance.

    6. Find the sample standard deviation ($s$) by taking the square root of your answer to part iv.

      Show Solution

      $\sqrt{24.29...}$.

      Answer: 4.93 minutes (approx)

  5. Compare the time taken by car users with non-car users (Mean=25, Median=24, Range=18, SD=6.2).

    Show Solution

    Comparison of Averages: Car users are faster on average (Mean 19 vs 25; Median 19 vs 24).
    Comparison of Spread: Car users have a more consistent journey time as their range and standard deviation are lower (Range 16 vs 18; SD 4.93 vs 6.2).

4. The dataset (5, 4, 7, 7, 8, 9, 4, 5, 4, 3) has a mean of 5.5 and a standard deviation of 1.96. Use this to find the mean and standard deviation of the following datasets:

  1. 5000, 4000, 7000, 7000, 8000, 9000, 4000, 5000, 4000, 3000

    Show Solution

    Each value has been multiplied by 1000.
    New Mean = $5.5 \times 1000 = 5500$.
    New SD = $1.96 \times 1000 = 1960$.

    Answer: Mean = 5500, SD = 1960

  2. 105, 104, 107, 107, 108, 109, 104, 105, 104, 103

    Show Solution

    100 has been added to each value.
    New Mean = $5.5 + 100 = 105.5$.
    Standard deviation is unaffected by addition.

    Answer: Mean = 105.5, SD = 1.96

  3. 5100, 4100, 7100, 7100, 8100, 9100, 4100, 5100, 4100, 3100

    Show Solution

    Each value has been multiplied by 1000, then 100 has been added.
    New Mean = $(5.5 \times 1000) + 100 = 5600$.
    Standard deviation is only affected by multiplication: $1.96 \times 1000 = 1960$.

    Answer: Mean = 5600, SD = 1960

  4. Use the same technique to find the mean and standard deviation of: 20000, 20000, 30000, 40000, 60000, 70000, 70000, 90000

    Show Solution

    Step 1: Code the data. Divide each value by 10000 to get a simpler dataset: 2, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 7, 9.
    Step 2: Find the mean of the coded data. $(2+2+3+4+6+7+7+9) \div 8 = 40 \div 8 = 5$.
    Step 3: Find the standard deviation of the coded data. $\sum(x-\bar{x})^2 = (2-5)^2 + ... + (9-5)^2 = 9+9+4+1+1+4+4+16=48$. Sample variance = $48 \div 7 \approx 6.857$. Coded SD = $\sqrt{48/7} \approx 2.619$.
    Step 4: Decode the results. Multiply the coded mean and SD by 10000.
    Final Mean = $5 \times 10000 = 50000$.
    Final SD = $2.619 \times 10000 = 26190$.

    Answer: Mean = 50000, SD = 26190 (approx)
